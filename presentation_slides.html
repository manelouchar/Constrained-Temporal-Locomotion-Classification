Problem & Motivation

Why does this matter?

  • Anticipation is Critical

    Exoskeletons and powered prostheses need to anticipate terrain changes before they happen.

  • Safety Risks

    A wrong transition → mechanical jolt → significant safety risk for the user.

  • Limitations of Current Systems

    Mostly rule-based, brittle, and fail to generalize across different subjects.

The Core Challenge

5 Locomotion Modes

LW (Level Walking)SA (Stair Ascent)SD (Stair Descent)RA (Ramp Ascent)RD (Ramp Descent)

  • Not all transitions are physically possible (e.g., no stair → ramp without flat walking).

  • Neural networks make fast, locally-coherent predictions but are temporally noisy.

Our Approach

Deep learning for recognition + symbolic filtering for temporal coherence

Key Design Decisions

Frame-wise Classification

The model outputs a prediction at every timestep, rather than just one label for the entire window, enabling high-resolution analysis.

Transition Mask

Logically restricts transitions: LW ↔ {SA, SD, RA, RD} only. Direct switches between stairs and ramps are forbidden.

Stability Constraints

Enforces a minimum duration (d_min) before accepting a state change to prevent rapid, physically impossible flickering.

Why Viterbi?

Raw argmax classifies each frame independently, which often produces illegal transitions (e.g., SA → RA) that are mechanically unsafe. Viterbi finds the most probable sequence of states over time.

Optimization Objective

s*=argmaxs1:T∑tℓ[t, st]+∑tτ(st-1, st)

ℓ [t, c]=log-softmax probability of class c at time t

τ (u, v)=0 if allowed, -∞ if forbidden

The Result

Illegal transitions are simply impossible in the decoded sequence. Output is guaranteed physically valid by construction.

Duration Filter

The Problem

Viterbi ensures valid transitions but allows rapid flickering (e.g., 20ms states), legal but physically impossible for human gait.

The Fix

Commit to state change only after dmin consecutive frames. Early-switch penalty.

switch accepted ⇔ count(scand) ≥ dmin

Offline Parameter Optimization

Strategy 1

Uniform Sweep

Single d* for all states. Minimizes false changes (FC) & latency.

d* = argmind[ 2 · FC/min + Lat / 500 ]
subject to: Acc(d) ≥ Accraw - 0.02
Result: 3 frames (30ms) for most subjects
Strategy 2

Per-State Optimization

Independent dmin(c) for each state. Same objective function.

Flat / Descent

5 frames

Ascent / Ramps

8 frames

Stair ascent needs more confirmation time.

Interpretation & Trade-offs

Recommended Strategy

Uniform Sweep offers the best balance: significant 22% reduction in false changes with minimal accuracy cost (<0.9%).

Optimization Risks

Per-state optimization reduces false changes by 45% but causes a 3% accuracy drop, violating our 2% constraint.

Latency Benefit

The uniform sweep strategy effectively halves the system latency (52ms → 26ms), improving real-time responsiveness.

Summary of Results

What we've achieved

  • Full LOSO pipeline: 10 subjects, strict protocol, no data leakage.

  • Robust Preprocessing: Normalization fitted on training folds only.

  • CNN-LSTM Deep: Top performance with 96.57% accuracy & 96.25% F1.

  • Efficiency: Best model is also fastest to train (11.1 min).

  • Viterbi + Uniform Sweep: -22% false changes with low latency (26ms).

  • Excellent Tradeoff: Stability gains with less than 1% accuracy cost.

  • Optimal Configuration: Window 2.5s, overlap 0.75, dropout 0.2 validated.
